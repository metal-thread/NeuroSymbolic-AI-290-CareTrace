# Test Scenario: 6-Year-Old with High Fever, Vomiting, and Lethargy

## Turn 1 (11:00 PM)

**Parent:**
My 6-year-old has a fever, threw up, and looks really wiped out. I’m worried.

**CareTrace Agent:**
I’ll help you decide what to do tonight. I need to check a few key things:
* What is the temperature?
* Is your child awake and responding normally?
* Are they drinking fluids?
* Any trouble breathing?
* Are there any known illnesses going around locally that you’re aware of?

---

## Turn 2 (11:02 PM)

**Parent:**
Temp is 103.5. He’s barely responding, just lying there. He doesn’t want to drink.
Also, there’s been a stomach virus going around his school this week.

**CareTrace Agent:**
Thank you, that’s important.

Right now I’m concerned about:
* High fever
* Reduced responsiveness
* Poor fluid intake

The local illness you mentioned can cause vomiting and fever, but I need one more key check:
* When did he last urinate?

---

## Turn 3 (11:04 PM)

**Parent:**
I don’t think he’s peed since this afternoon.

**CareTrace Agent:**
Based on what you’ve told me, this requires immediate evaluation in the ER.

**Reasons:**
* He is not responding normally
* He is not drinking fluids
* Possible dehydration (no urination for several hours)
* High fever

Even though a viral illness is going around locally, these symptoms are more severe and need urgent evaluation.

**What to do now:**
* Take him to the ER now
* Do not wait overnight

**If possible, bring:**
* Any medications given
* Temperature readings
